# Neon Gridlocked City Landscape

## Scene Description
An elevated three-quarter view over a dense downtown intersection at night, six lanes of traffic frozen in a gridlock — sedans, an SUV, and a box truck packed bumper-to-bumper across the frame, their windshields catching scattered neon reflections. Wet asphalt after recent rain doubles every light source into long vertical streaks. Glass-tower silhouettes rise on both sides of the frame, their windows a mix of dark and lit floors, receding into a hazy vanishing point at the upper third. A single traffic signal on a cantilevered arm glows amber in the mid-ground, slightly left of center, cable sagging with visible catenary curve. Steam rises from a street vent in the lower-right foreground, backlit by a magenta storefront sign, adding depth-separated atmosphere between foreground and skyline.

## Composition
Wide establishing shot, 16:9 horizontal, asymmetric framing — the traffic mass occupies the lower two-thirds weighted slightly right, skyline and negative sky space fill the upper third weighted left, avoiding a centered or mirror-symmetric arrangement. Camera height approximates a 6th-floor balcony looking down at roughly 35° declination — angled enough to read as atmosphere/backdrop, not a literal top-down board view (this must stay visually distinct from a flat top-down game grid). Foreground steam vent placed at lower-right per rule-of-thirds intersection as a depth anchor.

## Lighting
Blue hour, approximately 20 minutes after sunset — residual deep cobalt (#0B1B33) sky gradient at the top third transitioning to near-black at the horizon. Primary light sources are practical neon signage: cyan (#2DE2E6) and hot magenta (#FF2D95) storefront tubes at street level, plus warm amber (#FFB454) sodium streetlights spaced along the block, each casting a soft 1.5m-radius glow with visible falloff. Light direction is omnidirectional street-level (no single key light — this is a practical-lighting night scene), with hard vertical reflection streaks on the wet asphalt directly beneath each sign. Rim light from the cyan signage catches the near edge of the foreground vehicles' roofs.

## Depth & Scale
Four distinct depth layers: (1) foreground steam + one truck roof cropped at frame edge for scale reference, (2) mid-ground gridlocked traffic mass with a visible size gradient — nearer vehicles read as roughly 15% of frame height, vehicles near the vanishing point shrink to under 3%, (3) street-level storefronts and the traffic signal, (4) background tower silhouettes softened by atmospheric haze (desaturated toward #1A2438, contrast reduced ~40% vs foreground) to sell distance. No fog machine cliché — haze density increases gradually with distance, not as a uniform overlay.

## Atmosphere
Futuristic-but-grounded neon city mood — closer to a rain-soaked near-future metropolitan night than a clean sci-fi utopia. Believable urban textures: visible rust streaks on the truck's cargo box, a cracked windshield on one sedan, uneven light bulb color temperatures across different storefronts (not a uniform neon palette). The overall feeling should be "beautiful gridlock" — frustration and stillness rendered as something visually striking rather than grim, appropriate as an ambient backdrop behind a puzzle game UI that will be composited/dimmed on top of this scene later, so avoid placing any critical visual interest in the exact center third where UI would sit.
